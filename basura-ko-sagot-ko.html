BASURA MO ITAPON MO

PLASTIC KA, DESERVE MONG ITAPON DITO

The city government in partnership with the Department of Education formally launched “Basura Ko, Sagot Ko” last November 22 in a ceremony held at the People’s Astrodome after a motorcade along the main roads of the city.

The project basically aims to provide early training of children on proper waste segregation, disposal and management in response to the call for massive environmental protection and care.

Mayor Benjamin S. Lim in his message said his administration will soon tap other sectors like the non-government organizations, private sectors and the business people to join his campaign for a clean and healthy community but the city will have to start with the children.

“Umpisahan natin sa mga kabataan, sa mga estudyante baka sila na ang puedeng magturo sa kanilang mga magulang ng tamang pagpapahalaga sa kalikasan at pagtatapon ng basura,” said Lim.

Lim clarified that the city is not starting from zero from launching the project in his zero waste campaign. He said that when he assumed office his administration was able to improve the garbage collection of the city by urging and convincing the barangays to do segregation. He said he has been 70% successful in this aspect so far. .

“Lastly, we were able to fix our problem at the dumpsite which gave us more space so that over a period of three to four years, we will not have a problem in absorbing the waste,” he said.

Side by side with the launching is the staging of the Search for the Huwarang Estudyante; Responsable sa Organisadong Pamayanan or H.E.R.O. (individual and school categories) and the recital of the “Panata sa Pagmamahal sa Kalikasan” which is an oath to mother nature.

Winners will be recognized during the Educator’s Night this coming December during the city fiesta celebration. (CIO – Joseph C. Bacani)
