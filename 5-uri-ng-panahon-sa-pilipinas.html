3. Panuto: Sagutin ang mga tanong, (5 puntos)1. Sa iyong palagay, ano kaya ang uri ng pamahala mayroon angPilipinas sa Panahon ng mga Amerikano?​


Answer:

During the United States occupation of the Philippines (1898-1946), the United States government was in charge of providing education in the Philippines .

The impact of the American occupation on the public education system it has built in the country can be seen and felt. After the Americans took over Manila in May 1898, the establishment of public schools in the Philippines began. Initially, American soldiers first served as the first teachers who were later replaced by teachers from America. The largest group of teachers arrived in the Philippines in 1901 aboard the ship Thomas . These teachers were assigned to Manila and the provinces as Thomasites .

Under the law as 74, established the Department of Public Instruction ( Department of Public Instruction ). This law also made it possible for the country to have normal schools and trade schools such as the Philippine Normal School/University and the Philippine School of Arts and Trades now known as the Technological University of the Philippines.

In 1906 the colonial government sent Filipino scholars to America. They are called pensioners because the government funds their education. Many of them study education, law, medicine and engineering. When they returned to the Philippines, they became teachers and professors or servants in various branches of government.

In 1907, the Philippine Assembly passed the Gabaldon Act written by legislator Isauro Gabaldon of Nueva Ecija. Through this law, the construction of two public schools in each province was enacted. Many students are admitted to public schools because it is compulsory. English was used as the language of instruction.

Although free and compulsory education is not new to the Philippines because it was implemented during the Spanish period, the impact of the American program was stronger as it became wider in scope. Apart from tuition, books, pencils and paper are also free. In connection with this, the Americans also issued a law that can imprison parents who do not send their children to school. As a result of this policy, the percentage of citizens who learned to read and write increased and English immediately became the main language of education and journalism. Education also paved the way for the translation of Western knowledge on health care and hygiene to prevent disease.

Also during the American era, universities such as the University of the Philippines were established in the country in 1908. It became the center of secular education unlike the universities run by the Catholic Congregations.

In addition to the Catholic church, Americans have also allowed other religious sects to establish academic institutions. An example of this is the Protestants who founded Silliman University in Dumaguete on the island of Negros in 1901. Secular private universities such as Far Eastern University and University of Manila were also established .

It also provided an opportunity for the establishment of universities for women such as the Escuela de las Señoritas which is now known as Centro Escolar University and Philippine Women's University . It enables the emergence of female professionals in the fields of medicine, law and education.
