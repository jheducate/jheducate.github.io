7. Please explain this quote What is Wonderful about great literature is that it transforms the man who reads it towards the condition of the man who wrote.


Answer:

This quote highlights the transformative power of literature. It suggests that when a person reads great literature, they are not only entertained or informed, but they are also changed in some way. The idea is that by reading a great work of literature, a person begins to see the world in a new way and is transformed into a more enlightened and empathetic individual. The quote also emphasizes the connection between the reader and the writer, as the reader is transformed into a similar condition or state of mind as the writer. In this sense, literature has the ability to bridge the gap between individuals and bring people together through shared experiences and perspectives. This quote suggests that great literature has the power to uplift and transform individuals, making it a valuable and timeless form of art and expression.

20. Explain the quote: it takes a great Great deal of history to produce a little literature. Literature is a luxury, fiction is necessity Please I need help


Answer:

The quote "it takes a great deal of history to produce a little literature" refers to the idea that literature is a product of its historical context and cannot be divorced from the events, culture, and ideas of the time in which it was written. This means that a writer's experiences and the events that have shaped their lives and their world often find their way into their works and influence the themes and content of the literature they produce.

The second part of the quote, "Literature is a luxury, fiction is necessity," suggests that while literature may be seen as a luxury or an indulgence, fiction is an essential part of our lives. Fiction serves a vital role in helping us to understand and process our experiences and emotions, as well as helping us to see the world from different perspectives. This part of the quote implies that while literature may be seen as a form of entertainment, fiction is a necessity for our personal and emotional growth.
