Title: Elias' Monologue

Character: Elias

Setting: A forest in San Diego

Monologue:

I have lived in this forest for years, alone and in peace. The trees are my company, the animals are my friends, and the wind is my music. But I cannot ignore the world beyond the trees, the world that is filled with darkness and injustice.

My people suffer under the yoke of oppression, their spirits broken by the cruelty of those in power. The friars and the government officials have no compassion, no empathy for the plight of the poor. They only care about their own wealth and power, and they will stop at nothing to maintain their hold on society.

But there is hope. There are those who fight against the tyrants, who struggle to bring freedom and justice to our land. I have seen their courage, their determination, and I have joined their cause. I am willing to risk everything, even my own life, to see a brighter future for my people.

The road ahead will not be easy. The enemies of freedom are many, and they are powerful. But I know that our cause is just, and I know that we will prevail. We must stand together, we must fight together, and we must never give up hope.

For too long, our people have suffered in silence. But no more. We will rise up, we will speak out, and we will demand the justice that is rightfully ours. And when that day comes, we will know that we have won not just a battle, but a war for the soul of our nation.

(The monologue ends)

16. Is dramatic monologue/monologue considered acting?​

What is a dramatic monologue?

Dramatic monologue

refers to a type of poetry. These poems are dramatic in the sense that they have a theatrical quality; that is, the poem is meant to be read to an audience. To say that the poem is a monologue means that these are the words of one solitary speaker with no dialogue coming from any other characters. Think of one person standing alone on a stage speaking to an audience. Certainly, you are part of that audience, but the poem usually implies that the speaker is mainly talking to a specific person(s).

The Structure of a Dramatic Monologue

Shakespeare's

Photos.com/Photos.com/Getty Images

writer bio picture

By: Helen Fitzgerald

Updated April 12, 2017

A dramatic monologue is a long piece of writing or poetry that seeks to reveal the reader or character’s innermost thoughts and feelings. Dramatic monologues can be entire works on their own, such as Tennyson’s poem "Ulysses," or they can simply be passages within a longer work, such as the soliloquys in Shakespeare’s plays. The dramatic monologue as a form or literary device was popularized by English Romantic poets such as Percy B. Shelley and Robert Browning.
