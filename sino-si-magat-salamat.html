For a long time, not much was known about Magat Salamat outside of his participation in the Tondo Conspiracy. As a result, early Filipino historians such as Gregorio Zaide could only definitively say that he was born of noble heritage - presumably somewhere in or near Tondo.[4]

In Paragraph

Si Magat Salamat (1550-1595) ay ang prinsipe ng Kaharian ng Tondo at ang pangalawang anak ni Raha Lakandula. Ipinagpatuloy niya ang pag-aalsa na sinimulan ni Raha Lakandula ang kanyang ama ang himagsikan laban sa mga Kastila. Noong 1575 dumaong ang barko ni Martin de Goiti, isang mananakop na Espanyol upang makipag-usap sa hari ng Maynila na si Rajah Lakandula. Noong una ay nagkaroon ng maayos na negosasyon sa magkabilang panig nina Lakandula at Martin de Goiti ngunit di nagtagal ay nagkaroon ng isang mainit na labanan sa Bangkusay, Tondo. Sa pamumuno ni Martin de Goiti at mahigit 500 Espanyol natalo nila ang hukbo nina Raha Lakandula at Sulayman na may 100 Pilipino lamang. Muli silang nag organisa ng isang plano laban kay Martin de Goiti pero sa pagkakataong ito ay katulong na nila si Panday Pira, isang Pilipinong panday na gumagawa ng mga espada, kanyon at marami pang kagamitan na may talim na nagbabalak magtayo ng mga kanyon sa bukana ng Ilog de Maynila (Pasig River ngayon). Sa tulong ng mahigit 5000 Pilipinong nakibahagi sa himagsikan 137 lamang ang nasawi sa labanan. Nanalo ang panig nina Magat Salamat laban sa mga Espanyol. Namatay si Magat Salamat habang siya ay nahimbing sa kanyang pagtulog noong taong 1595. Bilang pagpupugay sa kanya isang mababang paaralan sa may Tondo ang ipinangalan sa kanya.

12. Lakan Dula, at salayman,magat salamat


Answer:

Lakan Dula (Baybayin: ᜎᜃᜈ᜔ ᜇᜓᜎ, Abecedario: Lácandólá) was the regnal name of the last Lakan (paramount ruler) of pre-colonial Tondo when the Spaniards first conquered the lands of the Pasig River delta in the Philippines in the 1570s.[2]

The firsthand account of Spanish Royal Notary Hernando Riquel[1]:13 says that he introduced himself to the Spanish as "Sibunao Lacandola", indicating for later Filipino historians that his given name was "Bunao".[3] However, the word Lakan which in current Tagalog form means gentleman, was a title equivalent to prince meaning he was Prince Dula. He later converted to Christianity and was baptised Carlos Lacandola.[4] Another common variation of the name is Gat Dula (alternatively spelled as a single word, Gatdula).[5] He is sometimes erroneously referred to as Rajah Lakandula, but the terms "Rajah" and "Lakan" have the same meaning, and in this domain the native Lakan title was used, making the use of both "Rajah" and "Lakandula" at the same time redundant and erroneous.[2]

Along with Rajah Matanda and Rajah Sulayman, he was one of three rajahs who played significant roles in the Spanish conquest of the Pasig River delta polities during the earliest days of the Philippines' Spanish colonial period.[3]

While it is unclear whether the entire name "Lakandula" represented a single titular name during his own lifetime, a few of his descendants in the first few generations after his death came to refer to themselves as the "Lakandula of Tondo", taking that name on as a noble title.[6]

26. bakit namuno si magat​


Answer:The Magat Salamat (1550-1595) was the prince of the Kingdom of Tondo and the second son of Rajah Lakandula. He continued the uprising that Raha Lakandula his father had started the revolt against the Spaniards. In 1575 the ship of Martin de Goiti, a Spanish conqueror, landed to talk to the king of Manila, Rajah Lakandula. At first there were good negotiations between Lakandula and Martin de Goiti but soon there was a heated battle in Bangkusay, Tondo. Under the leadership of Martin de Goiti and more than 500 Spaniards they defeated the army of Raha Lakandula and Sulayman with only 100 Filipinos. They again organized a plan against Martin de Goiti but this time they were assisted by Panday Pira, a Filipino blacksmith who made swords, cannon and many other bladed equipment that intend to build cannons at the mouth of the Manila River (now Pasig River). With the help of more than 5,000 Filipinos who took part in the uprising, only 137 people were killed in the fighting. Magat Salamat's side won against the Spaniards. Magat Salamat died while he was fast asleep in 1595. Explanation:

Magalat ay tubong Cagayan dahil sa tapang ni Magalat daan-daang mga sundalo at mga Pilipino ang lumaban sa kanya at sila ay napatay. Wala ni isa ang nag tagumpay sa kanila kay Magalat kasama ng mga Ilocano

• Gumawa ng paraan ang mga Espanyol kung paaano nila mapatay si Magalat , kaya naman sila ay kumuha ng Pilipino upang pumatay sa kanya.

• Ang grupo na pinamunuan kasama ng mga tagasunod ni Magalat ay na excile  sa Manila.

• Ngunit napawalang sala sila dahil sa  pagtulang ng mga Paring Dominiko na ma resulba ang problema.

• Nang siya ay makalaya ay muli siyang bumalik sa Cagayan at muli siyang nangkikayat na makisanib sap ag-aalsa.

• Ngunit sa kabila ng panghihikayat si Magalat sa mga Pilipino ay tumanggi sila sapagkat kanilang nabalitaan na nakagawa daw ng  mga kaluputan si magalat sa kanyang mga kapwa katutubo.

• Ngunit sa kabila noon ay naging matagumpay si Magalat nakontrol niya ang kanayunan at kalaunan ay Nakita ng mga Espanyol na nilulusob na sila ng Grupo ni Magalat.

• Noong malaman ng Gobernador Heneral na nagngangalang Francisco de Tello de Guzman na muling nag-aklas si Magalat ay kanyang ipinadala si Pedro de Chaves mula sa Maynila kasama ng mga tropang kolonyal kolonal sa Espana.

• Naging matagumpay ang laban ng Grupo ni de Chaves sa pakikipaglaban kay Magalat kanilang dinakip ang ilang kaalyado at lider ni Magalat.

• Si Magalat ay hindi napatay ng mga Espanyol kundi siya ay napatay ng kapwa niya Pilipino na kaalyado niya sa paglaban sa Espanyol sapagkat ito ay pingangakuan nila pabuya  o gantimpala kapag napatay si Magalat.
